Geography

Location

Argentina is located in the southern part of South America.

Countries, Regions, and Provinces

The countries surrounding Argentina are Chile, Bolivia, Uruguay, Brazil, and Paraguay.

Argentina is divided into 4 major regions:

  1. The Andes
  2. The North
  3. The Pampas
  4. Patagonia

Climate

Throughout Argentina, there are many varieties of climates. The northern part of the country is much warmer than the south. In the north, there is a mix of desert and subtropical forests where the climate is consistent throughout the year. The central part of the country has a temperate climate. During the summer, temperatures can exceed 104 degrees fahrenheit and in the winter reach below freezing point. The southern tip of Argentina, known as the Patagonia, has arid and polar conditions with well known glaciers such as Peritio Moreno. There is much precipitation as well as snowfall that never melts.

Physical Features

Argentina has a mix of different physical features. The Andes mountains range is located west of Argentina and runs north to south. There are also active volcanoes along these mountains bordering Chile. In addition, there are flat plains named Pampas with grasslands extending west to central Argentina.
